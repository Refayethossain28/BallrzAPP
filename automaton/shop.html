$5 deepManchester or Birmingham first? A one-page expansion call

Recommendation: Manchester first.

The case for Manchester. Manchester Airport is the UK's third-busiest (~28M passengers/yr) and — decisive for a premium transfer product — it serves long-haul routes (US, Gulf, Asia) that Birmingham largely lacks. Long-haul business passengers are the core buyer of a £95+ meet-and-greet transfer. The city also has a dense corporate base, major-event demand, and a hotel scene that supports partnership referrals.

The case for Birmingham — and why it waits. Birmingham is closer to London and has the NEC's exhibition traffic, but its airport skews short-haul/leisure, the premium ground-transport market is thinner, and proximity to London means some high-end demand already books London operators. A strong second city once the playbook is proven.

How to enter. Don't buy vehicles first: recruit 3–5 vetted owner-drivers on the existing 80% payout model, seed Manchester Airport transfers with a city-specific rate card, and spend 90 days on two channels — long-haul meet-and-greet, and account work with 10 target firms/hotels. Break-even ≈ 2 jobs/day per driver; review at day 90 before adding cars or a second city.
