What is this network training? A civil-war onset model — graded on years it has never seen.

A shared neural network learning from the real Fearon & Laitin (2003) dataset: 6,125 genuine country-years, 1945–1998, with seven covariates per row — population, regime score (polity2), GDP per capita, mountainous terrain, oil exports, ethnic fractionalisation and language count. It predicts whether a civil war began in that country-year. The consensus trains only on years ≤ 1988; everything after is held out, so the out-of-sample numbers below measure the model on a future it never saw and was never paid to fit. Honest limits: onsets are rare (1.65% of rows) and genuinely hard to predict — the model's value is risk ranking, not prophecy. Emission follows the 10-year schedule (2.3-year half-life, Bitcoin-style halvings, hard cap enforced by consensus); learning accrues between blocks, so whoever mines next collects everything released since the last block. The weights live in every block — the chain is the model.
