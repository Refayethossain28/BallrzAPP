Cortex · How to use it

A blockchain where mining trains a shared AI — and the coin, MIND, is minted for the learning you contribute.
⛏️ Mine👛 Wallet📈 Visual demo

The 60-second idea

Everyone shares one neural network. To mine a block you train it — your device runs real gradient descent until the network's error (loss) drops. The block carries the improved brain; anyone can verify it in one pass; you're paid MIND in proportion to the learning. No wasted hashes — the "work" is teaching.

1

Open the apps (or install them)

⛏️ Open Mine 👛 Open Wallet

Install on iPhone: open a page in Safari → tap Share → Add to Home Screen. You get two apps: a green icon (Mine) and a blue one (Wallet). Android/desktop: the browser's Install app option.

Both share one wallet and one chain on this device, and sync through the relay — so mine in one app, see the money in the other.

2

Mine your first block

On the Mine page, check the status line says ⇄ Connected, then tap the button:

⛏️ Mine a block (train & earn MIND)
⇄ Connected — mining is shared across the network.

"Training…" means your phone is literally training the AI: it starts from the network's current weights and runs ~300 rounds of gradient descent over the whole dataset. When the error has dropped enough, the improved weights are signed, sealed into a block, and broadcast. Watch model accuracy rise and loss fall — that's the shared brain learning. Your reward is proportional to the learning: early blocks pay more; when the model has learned everything it can, mining ends forever.

3

Check your wallet

0.2134 MIND · ⇄ networked
mined block #1+0.2134 MIND

The Wallet page shows your balance, your address (tap Copy address and share it to get paid), and every block you've mined or transfer you've made. Under Back up & restore, download your key encrypted with a passphrase — if you lose this device without a backup, the MIND is gone.

4

Send MIND to someone

Paste their address (starts with B), enter an amount, tap Send. The transfer is signed and broadcast, and shows as pending — it confirms when the next block is mined (by you or anyone). So: send, then mine one block to settle it.

5

Grow the network

Send anyone your Mine link — whoever opens it joins your chain: they'll see the blocks you mined, and their blocks appear on your screen. Every extra miner makes the shared model smarter, faster. A computer can join headlessly too:

RELAY=<your relay URL> npm run cortex:node

So where exactly is the AI training?

It's not a metaphor — the mining button runs a real training loop, on your device:

In the code: cortex/engine.js — trainStep() is one pass of backpropagation over every sample, train() loops it, and Chain.mineBlock() keeps training until the loss beats the previous block by the required margin. The model itself (a small multi-layer perceptron) lives on the chain: every block stores the full improved weights, so the blockchain is the AI's training history, checkpoint by checkpoint — cryptographically signed, verifiable by anyone, owned by everyone.

Validators never trust your claim: they re-run the network on the data (one forward pass) and recompute the loss from your published weights. You can't fake learning.

Honest notes

• This is a testnet — a real, working network, but the cryptography hasn't had an independent audit, so don't treat MIND as money yet (SECURITY.md has the full list).
• Your key lives in this browser/app. Back it up encrypted (Wallet → Back up & restore).
• An installed home-screen app has its own storage, separate from the Safari tab — each starts with a fresh wallet. Use Wallet → Reveal key → Import to move one wallet between them.
